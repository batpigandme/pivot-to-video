Lets make them long with tidyr::separate_rows(). Im also adding a numeric variable, identify (short for character(s) with whom I identify), and ensuring that respondent IDs dont get erroneously treated as numeric, by converting them to characters.

results <- results %>%
  tidyr::separate_rows(response) %>%
  dplyr::mutate(identify = 1,
                resp_id = as.character(resp_id))

Now well take one of the new tidyr verbs for a spin, pivot_wider(). Rather than fill things out with a bunch of NAs, well prepare our data to use with the UpSetR package by turning it into binaries, and ditch the respondent ID in the end.

I highly recommend Paul Campbells code-through using UpSetR, which gave me (among other things) the pro tip that upset() does not like tibbles (hence the as.data.frame() at the end).

binary_tib <- results %>%
  tidyr::pivot_wider(
    names_from = response,
    values_from = identify,
    values_fill = list(identify = 0)
  ) %>%
  dplyr::select(-resp_id)

binary_df <- as.data.frame(binary_tib)
UpSetR::upset(binary_df, nsets = 5, order.by = "freq")
